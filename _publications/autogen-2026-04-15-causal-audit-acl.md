abstract: |-
  Causal and intervention-based question answering is fundamental to advancing large language models toward reasoning beyond surface-level correlations and understanding underlying causal mechanisms. However, existing language model methods often rely on implicit language-level reasoning, which leads to opaque causal assumptions, unverifiable reasoning paths, and fragile predictions under complex interventions. This work proposes an explicit and auditable graph-based reasoning framework with target-aware causal graph construction and path-level causal evidence aggregation.
